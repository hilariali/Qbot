II. Scope and Precise Learning Outcomes (Compulsory Part, Module E: Social Implications)
The questions generated must be derived exclusively from the following topics and their specific learning outcomes, which comprise the 8 hours allocated to Module E:
1. Topic E.a. Technological Innovations (3 hours)
• LO: Understand the basic concepts of technological innovations and its applications.
    ◦ Examples include: pattern recognition through artificial intelligence (AI) and data science, 3D printing technologies, augmented reality (AR), and virtual reality (VR).
• (Note: Questions should focus on the concepts and applications; practical experience with these technologies is encouraged by the curriculum but generating questions demanding specific programming/technical implementation details is excluded).
2. Topic E.b. Health and Ethical Issues (3 hours)
• LO: Identify health hazards associated with the use of ICT, and propose good ergonomic practices when using them.
    ◦ Ergonomics consideration includes: reduction of injuries arising from repetitive strain injury (RSI), suitable design/placement of furniture, software design (user-friendliness, consistent user interface), and work environment.
• LO: Realise the importance of equity of access.
    ◦ Discussion should include: pros and cons of freedom of information on the Internet, equity issues in terms of the digital divide, gender equity, and access for the disabled (from local and global perspectives).
• LO: Discuss the ethical considerations on the use of ICT.
3. Topic E.c. Intellectual Property (2 hours)
• LO: Understand the basic ideas of intellectual property and copyright.
• LO: Understand the benefits and risks of different licensing schemes such as freeware, shareware, open source software and copyrighted software from the perspectives of users and software developers.
• LO: Relate acts of possible infringement of copyright in software and Internet piracy.
    ◦ Required discussion: social, legal, and economic implications of infringement, developing the habit of acknowledging sources, appropriate use of multimedia, and consequences of illegal uses/broadcasting.
• LO: Be aware of ways to reduce intellectual property theft on digital property (e.g., digital watermark and digital signature).
• LO: Know some of the legal consequences (especially in education) related to the infringement of copyright in Hong Kong.
